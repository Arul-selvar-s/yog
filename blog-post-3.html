Is Teeth Whitening Safe? Everything You Need to Know

By Dr. Yokesh Kumar R • April 2, 2026
Home› Blog› Is Teeth Whitening Safe? Everything You Need to Know
← Back to Blog

Why Patients Ask This Question

Teeth whitening is one of the most requested cosmetic dental treatments in India — and also one of the most misunderstood. Patients often come in after trying supermarket whitening products with disappointing or sometimes painful results, wondering if professional whitening is any different.

It is very different. Here's everything you should know before your first session.

How Does Professional Whitening Work?

Professional whitening uses a peroxide-based gel applied directly to your teeth. The peroxide penetrates the enamel and breaks down the coloured molecules responsible for stains — without damaging the tooth structure itself.

At Elysian Dental Lounge, we use in-clinic whitening which delivers significantly stronger and more uniform results than anything you can buy over the counter, in a single visit.

Fact: Over-the-counter whitening strips typically contain 3–10% peroxide. Professional in-clinic whitening uses 25–40% — with proper protection for your gums and sensitive areas.

Is It Safe?

Yes — when done by a qualified dentist. The key difference is that a dentist:

  • Examines your teeth and gums before starting (active cavities or gum disease must be treated first)
  • Applies protective barriers to your gums before the gel goes on
  • Controls the concentration and duration precisely for your specific case
  • Monitors you throughout the procedure

DIY kits and unregulated salon whitening lack all of these safeguards — which is why they sometimes cause sensitivity, gum burns, or uneven results.

Will It Make My Teeth Sensitive?

Some patients experience temporary sensitivity after whitening — usually lasting 24–48 hours. This is normal and resolves on its own. If you already have sensitive teeth, let us know beforehand — we can adjust the protocol to minimise this.

What Results Can You Expect?

Most patients see their teeth lighten by 4–8 shades in a single session. The exact result depends on the natural colour of your teeth and the type of staining.

  • Yellow stains from tea, coffee, or aging — respond very well
  • Brown stains from tobacco — take longer, may need multiple sessions
  • Grey stains from certain antibiotics — whitening may not be fully effective; veneers are a better option

How Long Does It Last?

With proper care, professional whitening lasts 1–3 years. To maintain results:

  • Avoid tea, coffee, red wine, and tobacco for 48 hours after treatment (the "white diet")
  • Use a whitening toothpaste 2–3 times per week for maintenance
  • Schedule a touch-up session annually if needed

Who Is Not Suitable for Whitening?

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding women
  • Patients with untreated cavities or active gum disease
  • Children under 16
  • Patients with crowns, veneers, or bridges on front teeth (whitening won't affect these restorations)
