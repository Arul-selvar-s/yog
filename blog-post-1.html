How to Keep Your Teeth Healthy Between Dental Visits

The Habits That Actually Make a Difference

Most patients visit us once or twice a year — but what you do in between those visits matters just as much as the visit itself. Dr. Yokesh Kumar has seen firsthand how small daily habits can either protect or slowly damage your teeth over time.

Key insight: Your toothbrush only cleans about 60% of your tooth surfaces. The rest is up to flossing and mouthwash.

Brushing — Are You Doing It Right?

Most people brush too hard and too fast. Here's what actually works:

  • Use a soft-bristled brush — medium or hard bristles damage enamel over time
  • Brush for a full 2 minutes, not 30 seconds
  • Use gentle, circular motions — not back-and-forth scrubbing
  • Hold the brush at a 45-degree angle to the gumline
  • Don't forget to brush your tongue — it harbours most of the bacteria that causes bad breath

Flossing — Non-Negotiable

No matter how well you brush, food and plaque build up between teeth where bristles can't reach. Flossing once a day removes this before it hardens into tartar — which can only be removed by a dentist.

  • Floss before brushing, not after — this loosens debris so brushing can sweep it away
  • Use about 18 inches of floss, using a fresh section for each tooth
  • If flossing irritates your gums initially, continue anyway — it will stop within a week as your gums strengthen

What You Eat and Drink

Your diet is one of the biggest factors in oral health. Certain foods actively protect your teeth while others quietly erode them.

Foods that protect your teeth:

  • Dairy (cheese, milk, curd) — rich in calcium which strengthens enamel
  • Crunchy vegetables (carrots, celery) — naturally scrub tooth surfaces
  • Green tea — contains polyphenols that fight bacteria
  • Water — especially fluoridated water, which actively protects enamel

Foods to limit:

  • Sugary drinks and sodas — the acid erodes enamel within minutes
  • Sticky sweets like toffee — cling to teeth long after eating
  • Citrus fruits in excess — acidic, best consumed with meals, not alone
  • Coffee and tea — stain teeth over time (drink water after)

When to Visit Us

Even with perfect home care, professional cleaning every 6 months is essential. Tartar buildup can only be removed with professional tools. Additionally, early cavities have no symptoms — only an X-ray can spot them before they become painful.

Don't wait for pain to visit a dentist. By the time a tooth hurts, the problem is almost always more serious than it would have been if caught earlier.
